What to Expect

Kattappurathu is an old bungalow facing the Main Central Road, in the quaint little village of Ezhinjillam (colloquially known as Idinjillam).
The property, though not very large, is teeming with life. Local fruit trees like mango, jack fruit, flowering plants that attract butterflies and dragon flies, a flurry of native birds (including, recently, a visiting peahen), squirrels, an odd Iguania, a family of mongoose and a few proud Indian monitor lizards add up to the ‘life’ in the property. Fish grown in a pond within the land and a few varieties of poultry are treated as part of the family. The house overlooks paddy fields of Kuttanadu. In the fields and farm area behind the property, one may encounter a few turtles, owls and beetles. We do encourage our guests to explore our extended property and enjoy nature at its best. We take pride in being a green oasis in the middle of a fast developing region, try to celebrate life, and take care not to harm the animals that make our place their home.

We offer the staying quarters for our guests on the top floor of our house. The top floor, with two quarters, common areas and common kitchen, is self contained, with independent access and provides relative privacy from our home downstairs.

Apart from the bungalow, we have an adjacent free standing wooden granary (see the facing picture) that brings nostalgic memories of a grand era of farming in Kuttanad. Till recently we used to keep paddy cultivated in our fields in this granary. Now, we maintain it for our guest to see and our next generation to remember the toil of our ancestors.

We invite you to visit us with an open mind, be a Kuttanad farmer in spirit and savour in the sounds, smells, sights and experiences of Central Tranvancore, while staying with us as our guests.

Native Sights

We offer the warmth of a home on the banks of extensive paddy fields, where one can witness the kuttanadan sun setting into endless expanse of paddy fields behind coconut trees. An old ara or grannary on the property is unique to the region. A mansion built in the 60s inspired by art deco completes the sights. The larger of our boarding rooms have extensive windows to the paddy fields, which also brings in a light breeze into the house.

Native Flora Fauna

We like to think of our home as a green oasis. If you look beyond the two overarching mango trees, themselves perhaps as old as India herself, you shall find jackfruits, berries, plantain, banana and tamarinds. The property is also home to an eclectic mix of animals and birds. We would encourage you to slow down, savour in the sights and smells that are on offer at our home.

Calm & Quiet

The property is right on the main central road in Kerala, but cocooned in the tiny village of Ezhinjillam. This allows you a quiet peaceful slice of Kerala, away from the hustle of a routine Kerala. And if you are indeed feeling the urge to experience the hustle, we encourage you to visit the old markets, steeped in history, with its own canal system, in Changanacherry

Aranmula

the mirror that isn’t glass.

Aranmula, 23 kms from Idinjillam, is famous for Aranmula Kannadi (Aranmula Mirror), a unique handicraft product that is believed to have existed even during the Vedic period. The myth about its origin and its secret composition make it a truly fascinating product. It is made up of some undisclosed metals alloyed with silver, bronze, copper and tin. It is a precious inherited gift. The aura of secret behind its manufacture makes it all the more famous. Tradition links this craft closely with the ancient Aranmula Parthsarathy temple. Any write up on Aranmula will be imperfect without a mention of Plliodams, vachippattu and vallasadya. Palliyodams are Aranmula’s unique snake boats (Chundan Vallam) which devotees hold in reverence, considering it as the divine vessel of the presiding deity in Sree Parthasarathy temple. Sitting two in row along the length of the Boat the oarsmen will row in rhythm of the vanchipattu (boatman’s song) being sung by a group of experienced singers. Vallasadya is a ritualistic feast, done as a vazhipadu (offering) by the devotees to Lord Krishna, the presiding deity at the Sree Parthasarathy temple. It lasts for more than sixty days and with more than sixty exquisite dishes to relish. As part of the Vallasadya the crew of the selected snake-boat will be accorded a customary reception on their arrival to the temple ghat. They circumambulate the temple, singing Vanchippattu, songs of the boatmen in praise of Lord Parthasarathy, before being led to the temple Oottupura (dining hall) for the feast. As the feast progresses, the guests demand more rice and dishes by singing in the same style as that of Vanchippattu. This generates an ambience filled with fun, hurried activities and a flow of energy that is unique to Vallasadya. As many as 50 to 64 items are usually served during the course of this sumptuous feast. It is conducted usually during July-September every year.

Thiruvalla

A hotspot of art and culture

We belong to Thiruvalla, in Pathanamthitta District. The name Thiruvalla is a colloquial form of Sreevallabhapuram, named after the chief deity Sree Vallabha of Sreevallabha Temple here. It is a highly orthodox Hindu Temple dedicated to Purusha as Lord Sreevallabhan. It is one among the oldest and biggest Temples of Kerala and a major destination for devotees all over India for centuries. This is the only temple in Kerala where Kathakali, the folk art of Kerala, is performed as a ritual offering every night. Thiruvalla is famous for many other things also. It is the headquarters of the Malankara Marthoma Syrian Church that conducts the Maramon Convention, one of the largest Christian conventions in Asia at Maramon near Thiruvlla annually. Again, the Paliakara Church in Thiruvlla has some exquisite mural paintings on the walls of its sanctum sanatorium. Chakkulathukavu Sree Bhagavathy temple, where large number of women devotees assemble annully once for conducting pongla ritul is situated on the border of Pathanamthitta and Alappuzha districts at Neerettupuram.

Pallathuruthy

The Houseboat town in Kuttanadu.

Pallathuruthy, famous for house boats is only 23 kms from our place. Different types of House Boats are available here to suit the purse of the tourist. Most of the house boats serve local delicacies like ‘Kappayum meen vevichathum’ (Tapioca and a special fish curry using kudampuli- an exclusive spice of Kuttanadu) and Karimeen pollichathu (A special fry of Pearlspot packed in banana leaves giving the preparation a rich aroma that arouses taste buds).

Konni Elephant Camp

Where elephant boys become elephant men

Konni, a forested village in the State of Kerala in South India, situated on the bank of the mighty Achencoil River has a long and rich history of man-forest interface. Vast expanse of forests of Western Ghats in the background is crisscrossed by numerous streams and rivulets, which add to the exceptional scenic beauty of the landscape. Konni Eco Tourism Project envisages developing visitor locations in the forest tracts of Konni-Achencoil forests and developing Konni Elephant Camp as a facilitation centre. Elephant is at the nucleus of the entire project. The facilities developed at Konni include elephant viewing, elephant feeding, elephant ride, watching elephant bath etc. An elephant museum, which is planned to be developed as a complete natural history museum based on elephant, also is set up. The elephant camp at Konni serves as a hub of forest-based eco tourism activities in southern Kerala. Various locations in forest areas also have been developed, which can be accessed through trekking in forests.
